## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Visual: PASSES.** `.gradient-text` neutralized to solid pink; glows/pulse/grid gone; single 7%-alpha hero wash; no hero-metric template; one eyebrow site-wide; clean at 390px. Residual: the section *scaffold* (hero → trust bar → cards → pricing → FAQ → CTA) is still the standard SaaS skeleton — saved by content, not structure.

**Copy voice: PARTIAL FAIL — the strongest remaining AI tell.** 111 em-dash lines; worse than the count is the construction monoculture: nearly every subhead is [concrete list] — [payoff clause], and "X, not Y" recurs 4+ times ("does the work, not just the reporting"; "before the due date, not after the fine" — which also appears twice for the same feature on two pages). The sass lands where it's specific ("before they ghost you", "without the Sunday spreadsheet", "no duct tape between four tools") and dies in the FAQ ("Absolutely. We use enterprise-grade encryption…") — exactly where a skeptic lingers longest.

**Deterministic scan (B):** CLI clean (exit 0). Browser detector on fresh build: 15 findings / 4 pages — all 6 `low-contrast` hits are **verified false positives** (detector reads the 7%-alpha gradient stop at full opacity; actual blend ≈6.7:1, passes AA); `single-font: poppins` ×4 is the deliberate brand choice; **`nested-cards` ×5 is the one real recurring finding** (pricing ×2, others ×1).

## Overall Impression

The redesign held: visually clean of slop, honestly positioned, vertically specific. What remains is a copy-editing problem (rhythm monoculture + one true claims contradiction) and pricing-page decision friction — one pass, not another overhaul.

## What's Working

1. **Domain specificity as a trust weapon** — WH-347/Taft-Hartley/NACHA copy no template produces.
2. **Honest beta positioning** — beta framed as leverage ("your feedback ships in days"), no fake logos.
3. **Disciplined effect layer** — every 2024-era effect class deliberately neutralized in CSS; real screenshots in a zigzag with an asymmetric break.

## Priority Issues

1. **[P1] Integration claims contradict across pages** — /features sells "Connect Stripe, HubSpot, Salesforce, and your help desk once" as live; FAQ says HubSpot/Salesforce/Jira are "rolling out to beta customers now" (features/page.tsx:269-271 vs FAQ.tsx:34). This is the site's own named anti-reference (claims outrunning product). **Fix:** align features copy to "Stripe and Slack today; HubSpot & Salesforce rolling out in beta." → `/impeccable clarify`
2. **[P1] Copy rhythm monoculture** — em-dash pivot + "X, not Y" reflex on nearly every subhead; duplicate deadline/scramble line on two pages. **Fix:** editing pass — no two adjacent sections share the [list]—[payoff] template; max one "X, not Y" per page; extend the persona-line voice into the FAQ. → `/impeccable clarify`
3. **[P2] /pricing has no metadata** — client component inherits the homepage title; wrong in tabs/bookmarks/SERP for the highest-intent page. **Fix:** server page.tsx with metadata + client child, mirroring features/. → `/impeccable polish`
4. **[P2] Pricing decision friction** — undefined bullets ("All modules", "Smart guidance"), "Advanced analytics" duplicated on Growth AND Scale (page.tsx:204,217), 3 tiers × 2 intervals × union add-on bands at the money moment. **Fix:** compact comparison table or tooltips; differentiate or drop the duplicated bullet. → `/impeccable clarify` + `/impeccable polish`
5. **[P3] Dead weight + ambiguity** — "Under the hood" tall card mostly empty gradient; sales vs consulting links undifferentiated; nested-cards ×5 (detector-confirmed). → `/impeccable polish`

## Minor Observations

- FAQ compliance answer describes a vaguer product than the pricing page's precise union add-on — same feature, two altitudes.
- "For businesses that mean business" is the weakest sass attempt; the persona lines earn the register honestly, the pun reaches for it.
- No /#anchor links left in the footer — subpage anchor breakage risk eliminated.
- Trust load rests entirely on screenshots + candor: zero testimonials or outcome numbers anywhere (known constraint in beta).

## Questions to Consider

1. The pitch is "agents that *do* the work" — yet no page shows one finished artifact (a drafted follow-up, a filled WH-347). Why is the proof always a dashboard, never an output?
2. If the FAQ is where the skeptic lingers, why is it the one section written by the corporate ghost instead of the voice that wrote "before they ghost you"?
3. Growth is 3× Starter, but the site never prices the pain (one missed prevailing-wage filing, one churned retainer). Where's the number that makes $149/mo look free?
