## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Not slop.** /unions "is the least sloppy page on the site" — "Retro pay runs in one pass, not a weekend," "backed by a card on file," "Bring us your CBA" pass the could-only-be-SaaSy test. Verified fresh: -v3 clips play populated data ($20,625 MRR, named accounts — no $0 ghosts); trial wording unified; AI-training FAQ present; /unions pricing matches /pricing exactly. Residue: the "counting tools" tic ×3 ("four tools" / "7 tools" / "six tabs") and single-word gradient-text keywords (in-palette, defensible, but should be owned as a decision).

**Deterministic scan (B):** CLI clean. Browser: 17 findings / 4 pages — low-contrast ×8 re-verified as the known alpha-stop false positive (~5.7:1 actual, passes AA); single-font ×4 deliberate; nested-cards ×4 shared component; **1 new real finding: ~94ch line-length block on /unions**.

## Priority Issues

1. **[P1] /unions has zero product evidence** — text-only page for the most skeptical audience; violates PRODUCT.md Principle 1. Fix: one mid-page visual (WH-347 render, per-fund remittance report, or CBA scale table) — needs a product-surface screenshot session.
2. **[P1] Audience split-identity on /unions** — hero says "unionized employers and contractors" but it's linked from the "Unions & labor organizations" card. Dues checkoff is the employer side; a local receives/reconciles. Fix: explicit dual-audience framing.
3. **[P2] Jira drift** — FAQ lists Jira in rollout; /features prose and JSON-LD don't. One canonical list.
4. **[P2] Counting-tools tic ×3** — rewrite two.
5. **[P3] hero-loop-v3 title card** says "early access open" (baked in; next re-render).
6. **[P3] /unions total-cost opacity** — never states the Growth base price next to the add-on.

## Strengths

1. /unions copy is real domain expertise weaponized as brand voice; money-handling FAQ answered first is exactly right.
2. Accessibility follow-through, not checkbox — SSR poster default, reduced-motion branch, 44px focus-visible pause.
3. Consistency engineering — content-addressed media with the cache lesson documented, pricing parity across 3 surfaces, correct sitemap/OG/canonical on a new route.

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan/Riley/Casey: no substantive flags** (Riley confirms everything resolves). **Pat (union office manager):** can't tell if the page is for her local or her contractors (P1); **no migration story** for 20 years of member rosters/authorization records — her biggest switching fear; no screenshot to judge friendliness; inherited "open beta" framing reads as audit risk.

## Questions to Consider

1. "Bring us your CBA" is the best CTA on the site — why is it a sales form instead of an actual CBA upload that triggers the promised same-day quote?
2. If union compliance is the wedge, why does "WH-347 certified payroll software" search intent land on a health-scoring homepage? Is /unions a page, or secretly the company?
3. Does /unions need its own maturity signal (locals served, dues volume remitted) to override the beta framing it inherits from shared chrome?
